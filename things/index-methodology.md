# Index methodology

An index methodology is the [[rule-book]] for a specific index. It specifies eligibility tests, weighting scheme, [[index-rebalancing]] schedule, and the treatment of corporate actions.

Methodologies fall into two camps. Rules-based systems pick constituents by mechanical screens; outcomes are reproducible and predictable. Discretionary systems let a committee step in when judgment matters. Most indexes sit between the two: hard rules with narrow committee discretion.
